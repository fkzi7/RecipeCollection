Here are two main course recipes with all the details: --- ### 1. **Grilled Lemon Herb Chicken with Roasted Vegetables** #### **Ingredients:** - **For the Chicken:** - 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts - 2 tablespoons olive oil - 2 cloves garlic, minced - Zest and juice of 1 lemon - 1 tablespoon fresh thyme (or 1 tsp dried) - 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary (or 1 tsp dried) - Salt and pepper to taste - **For the Roasted Vegetables:** - 2 medium potatoes, diced - 1 zucchini, sliced - 1 red bell pepper, chopped - 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved - 2 tablespoons olive oil - 1 teaspoon dried oregano - Salt and pepper to taste #### **Instructions:** 1. **Marinate the Chicken:** - In a bowl, combine olive oil, minced garlic, lemon zest and juice, thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper. - Place the chicken breasts in a resealable bag or shallow dish, and pour the marinade over the chicken. Seal and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes (up to 2 hours). 2. **Prepare the Vegetables:** - Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). - In a large bowl, toss the diced potatoes, zucchini, red bell pepper, and cherry tomatoes with olive oil, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. - Spread the vegetables out in a single layer on a baking sheet. - Roast in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and golden, stirring halfway through. 3. **Grill the Chicken:** - Preheat your grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. - Remove the chicken from the marinade and grill for 6-7 minutes on each side or until fully cooked (internal temperature should reach 165°F/75°C). - Remove the chicken from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes. 4. **Serve:** - Serve the grilled chicken alongside the roasted vegetables. #### **Cooking Time:** - **Prep Time:** 15 minutes (for marinade and vegetables) - **Marinate Time:** 30 minutes (optional: up to 2 hours) - **Cook Time:** 25-30 minutes for vegetables, 12-14 minutes for chicken - **Total Time:** 1 hour (if marinating for 30 minutes) --- ### 2. **Beef Stir-Fry with Vegetables** #### **Ingredients:** - 1 lb (450g) flank steak or sirloin, thinly sliced against the grain - 2 tablespoons soy sauce - 1 tablespoon cornstarch - 2 tablespoons vegetable oil (divided) - 1 red bell pepper, sliced - 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced - 1 medium onion, thinly sliced - 2 cloves garlic, minced - 1-inch piece fresh ginger, minced - 1 cup snap peas or snow peas - 2 tablespoons soy sauce (for stir-fry sauce) - 1 tablespoon oyster sauce - 1 tablespoon rice vinegar - 1 teaspoon sesame oil - Cooked rice or noodles, for serving #### **Instructions:** 1. **Marinate the Beef:** - In a small bowl, combine the beef slices with 2 tablespoons soy sauce and 1 tablespoon cornstarch. Toss until the beef is well-coated, and let it marinate for 15-20 minutes. 2. **Prepare the Stir-Fry Sauce:** - In a separate bowl, whisk together 2 tablespoons soy sauce, oyster sauce, rice vinegar, and sesame oil. Set aside. 3. **Stir-Fry the Beef:** - Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. - Add the marinated beef in a single layer and cook for 2-3 minutes without stirring. Flip the beef and cook for another 1-2 minutes until browned but not overcooked. Remove the beef from the pan and set aside. 4. **Cook the Vegetables:** - In the same pan, add the remaining 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil. - Add the onion, garlic, and ginger, and sauté for 1-2 minutes until fragrant. - Add the bell peppers and snap peas, and stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until they begin to soften but remain crisp. 5. **Combine and Finish:** - Return the cooked beef to the pan along with the prepared stir-fry sauce. Stir to combine and cook for another 2 minutes until everything is well-coated and heated through. 6. **Serve:** - Serve the beef stir-fry over cooked rice or noodles. #### **Cooking Time:** - **Prep Time:** 10 minutes - **Cook Time:** 10 minutes - **Total Time:** 20 minutes --- These two dishes offer a variety of flavors and are quick to prepare.
